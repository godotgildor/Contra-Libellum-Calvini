A few things worth flagging for your review, all collected in the Translator's Notes:
The two genuinely doubtful spots are "Calvire" (Calvin §before — in the Benevole Lector, "publicis tijpis extenuare et Calvire, defendere") and the "Deest" lacuna in Vaticanus's response to Calvin §13. Both look like exactly the marginalia-interleave failure mode you've documented — "Calvire" reads like an intrusive gloss punning on Calvin's name, and "Deest" is almost certainly an editorial "[text missing]" notation read inline rather than part of Castellio's sentence. These are the two highest-priority scan spot-checks.
Two identifications I made but couldn't fully confirm: "Hieronymo" in §34 I read as Jérôme Bolsec (the predestination antagonist at Geneva) rather than St. Jerome, and "autochatachritos" as garbled Greek αὐτοκατάκριτος / self-condemned (Titus 3:11), which Castellio or the compositor seems to have set in Latin letters rather than Greek type — worth checking whether the original uses Greek script there.
The title-page date "M. D. L. C. XII" is malformed as a numeral but unambiguously 1612 from the colophon and the internal "1553... last year" reference, so I rendered it 1612 silently and noted it.
One structural note matching your memory: I kept the Benevole Lector (editor's, third-person about Castellio) and the Praefatio (Castellio's own, first-person) formally distinct, since the prompt's pilot already established that the prefatory address is the Dutch editor's work.
